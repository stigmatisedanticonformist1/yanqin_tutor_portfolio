Personalised One‑to‑One Lessons

Lessons are conducted one‑to‑one so that each session focuses on the student's individual weak points. Each lesson follows a structured cycle designed to diagnose gaps, strengthen understanding, and extend the student towards harder examination questions.

1. Diagnose Weak Points

At the start of a topic, I test the student with targeted questions to identify specific conceptual gaps or recurring mistakes.

2. Guided Practice

Students attempt carefully selected questions so I can observe their thinking process and pinpoint misunderstandings.

3. Clear Explanation

I explain the underlying concepts step‑by‑step, focusing on the reasoning behind each method rather than memorising procedures.

4. Harder Exam Questions

Students then tackle more challenging exam‑style problems to explore deeper applications of the concept.

5. Homework for Reinforcement

Additional problems are assigned as homework so that the student can consolidate the ideas independently before the next lesson.

Insights on Preparing for A‑Levels

Having gone through the A‑Level system myself, I emphasise study strategies that maximise understanding and efficiency rather than blind memorisation or excessive revision.

1. Target the Cambridge Curriculum

Instead of revising randomly, preparation should focus on the structure of the Cambridge syllabus and the types of questions that consistently appear in examinations.

2. Master Fundamentals First

Strong basics make difficult questions much easier. I emphasise conceptual understanding before moving on to harder problems.

3. Time Management

Students learn how much time to allocate to each question so they avoid getting stuck and can complete the paper efficiently.

4. Efficient Answering Techniques

Exams reward clear working and structured answers. I teach techniques that help students score the most marks with the least unnecessary effort.
